Calvin Brown

Urban Planner, Data Analyst, Cartographer, Transit and Safe Streets Advocate

About

I am an urban planner, civic data analyst, and cartographer. I leverage data and spatial analytics to visualize and communicate key insights about the built environment and the impacts of future projects, regulations, and scenarios on communities. I build tools and create compelling graphics to make data more accessible, inform decision-making, and empower communities. I have an extensive background in community outreach and advocacy, and am fluent in Spanish.

Recent Projects

Visualizing Travel Behavior Data Using Python and flowmap.blue
I developed a script that intakes Replica origin/destination data, processes it, and creates an output that is then plugged directly into flowmap.blue to visualize the origin/destination travel flows of over nine million average weekday trips within Alameda County.
Envisioning Calmer Intersections for New York City
I envisioned small, pragmatic adjustments and calming measures to mitigate the severe safety at the intersection of Queens Boulevard, Van Dam Street, and Thomson Avenue.
Safer Streets for East Long Island City
I developed a set of recommendations that address the traffic and pedestrian safety issues in the area surrounding LaGuardia Community College.
33rd Street Sky Center
I envisioned a public-private transit-oriented development that mitigates a number of transportation and quality of life issues at 33rd St. - Rawson St. 7 line stop.
